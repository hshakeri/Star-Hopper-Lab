⭐ Star Hopper Lab

A one-page guide for parents · ages 9–16 · free · no accounts, no ads, no timers

What is it?

A space platformer that is secretly a science lab. Your kid pilots a small robot, and every jump is automatically logged as a data point. Each world runs on a hidden rule — your kid uncovers it from data they collect themselves, then proves it with a prediction. They also write real (tiny) programs in KidCode to run their experiments.

What is my kid actually learning?

  • Coding: variables, loops, conditionals, and tiny prediction formulas — typed and run for real.
  • Data science: measuring carefully, reading charts, averaging repeated trials, fitting patterns, fair sampling, and knowing when a conclusion is solid.
  • Science habits: predict before you test, replicate before you conclude, check weird data points, and say honestly where your model stops working.

Is it healthy screen time?

No ads, no accounts, no purchases, no leaderboards, no streaks, no notifications. Progress saves only on your device. Each mission ends with a natural stopping point — a published "journal article" your kid can show you. It even works offline after the first visit.

How to be part of it (two minutes)

  • Ask: "What was the hidden rule? How did you catch it?" — the museum keeps their published findings.
  • Ask: "What did you predict, and what really happened?" — being close counts; being honest counts more.
  • Let them show you a "Rule Card" — it has their own chart on it.

Star Hopper Lab is free and open source. No data ever leaves your device.
